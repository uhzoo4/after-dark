The Story

It started with one song at 3am. I don't remember which one. But I remember the feeling — like the music wasn't playing through speakers but through the walls of the room, through my chest. That's when I knew this wasn't background noise. This was the closest thing I had to a language that said what I couldn't.

The Weeknd taught me that darkness doesn't need to be explained. It just needs to be felt. After Hours wasn't sad. It was honest. There's a difference. Lana Del Rey gave that darkness texture — a film grain, a slow exhale, the kind of beauty that only exists because it knows it won't last.

I listen to music the way some people journal. Privately, obsessively, always at night. This page is that journal — made visible.

Lana Del Rey

Cinematic sadness with a vintage lens. Lana sounds like a place that doesn't exist anymore but you remember perfectly. Born to Die hit different every single time because she isn't performing the emotion — she's reconstructing it.

Favourite album — Born to Die
